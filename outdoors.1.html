Southbank south

The south bank of the river Thames, between Tower Bridge and Westminster Bridge, is a fantastic walk. On (or very near the route) are the Globe Theatre, the Tate Modern art gallery, Southwark Cathedral, the London Eye, Borough Market and an outdoor book market.

Epping Forest very far east

Old trees! You can take the central line out here — it's a bit of a trek but it's beautiful. It has a 350-year-old oak tree which is very impressive (unless you're a Sequoia).

Greenwich far south (east)

A big hub for London's naval history. Has the Cutty Sark (a tea clipper, now a museum) and the old Naval College (designed by Christopher Wren) but, most importantly, the Observatory at the top of the hill which defines Greenwich Mean Time. You can ake the boat there — the Thames Clipper!

Location
Kew Gardens west

London's biggest and most extensive botanic garden. A beautiful and peaceful place. The Maid of Honour, just outside the park, has a very old school tea room and is famous for one of its pastries.
